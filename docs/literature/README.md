## What the Scan Looked For

The search process focused on identifying work involving:

- Geometric or spatial modeling of questions
- Entropy applied to interrogative structure (not responses)
- Deterministic representations of question form
- Use of classical interrogatives (Who, What, When, Where, Why, How) as formal dimensions
- Question-as-program or symbolic interrogative systems
- Information-theoretic approaches to question selection

## Summary (From the Report)

The report found:

- No reviewed studies implement a six-dimensional field over classical interrogatives.
- No reviewed studies compute entropy over interrogative form instead of response distributions.
- No reviewed studies use deterministic spatial injections of questions into a geometric field.

Several related approaches were identified in adjacent research areas (e.g., information gain,
question-as-program modeling, symbolic reasoning evaluation), but none employed the geometric
architecture described in this repository.
